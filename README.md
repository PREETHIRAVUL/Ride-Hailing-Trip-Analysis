Ride-Hailing Trip Analysis

1.Business Problem
Uber needed better visibility into trip demand, revenue performance, and operational efficiency across locations, vehicle types, and payment methods. The lack of centralized insights into booking patterns, trip completion efficiency, and customer behavior made it difficult to optimize resource allocation, improve service quality, and maximize revenue growth.

2.Objective

Analyze booking trends, trip efficiency, revenue patterns, and customer payment behavior to identify operational improvement opportunities and support data-driven decision-making.

6. Key Insights
Uber completed 103.7K bookings, generating approximately $1.6M revenue with an average booking value of $15 per trip
Peak demand occurred during 8–10 AM and 6–9 PM, indicating strong commuter-driven ride patterns
UberX generated the highest booking volume and overall revenue contribution among all vehicle categories
Digital payments accounted for nearly 67% of total transactions, reflecting strong customer adoption of cashless payments
Most trips were short-distance urban rides, while long-distance trips generated significantly higher fare value
Penn Station / Madison Square Garden emerged as the highest-demand pickup location across the network

8. Recommendations
Optimize driver allocation during peak demand hours and high-traffic pickup zones
Refine surge pricing strategies during evening and night-time demand periods
Increase focus on digital payment adoption to improve operational efficiency
Promote high-performing vehicle categories to maximize revenue contribution
Use trip distance and location insights to improve ride distribution and pricing strategies
